# ValueChainHackers

ValueChainHackers is an open lab and community where we hack supply chains.  

We take on real problems. We bring together students, researchers, companies, and communities. We map what is broken, we build and test new ways of working, and we help others do the same.

Hacking means looking at how things really work. It means finding the points where you can change the system. We do this through applied learning, working with people in the field, building prototypes, and using modern tools like AI and network analysis.

It is not about theory. It is about helping people get the skills, the urgency, and the community they need to drive real change. 

Our stack is open source. Anyone can use it. Anyone can join.

## The problem

Supply chains are full of wicked problems.  

To fix them we need solutions that are:
- socially and environmentally impactful
- financially viable
- scalable in practice  

Many want to help but lack structure or support.  

There is no coordination between businesses, institutions, and communities. That is what we work on.


## How we work

We run a 20-week applied program.  

In that time people go from real-world problem to working solution.  
We work with businesses, communities, and researchers.  

The program includes:
- Real-world challenges 
- Business and community engagement  
- Mentorship 
- Applied learning with tools and tech  

We help people build:
- Agency (I know what to do)
- Urgency (I know why it matters)
- Community (I know I am not alone)


## What we build

We give teams tools to:
- Map supplier relationships
- Analyze supply chain networks
- Automate research tasks with AI
- Visualize complex chains
- Build solutions people can actually use


## What’s inside

- AI chat and RAG: Ollama, OpenWebUI  
- Automation: n8n  
- Data and dashboards: Grafana, Kotaemon, JupyterHub, RStudio  
- Graph database: Neo4j  
- Search and embeddings: Qdrant, SearXNG  
- Routing: Traefik

Also:
- Projects from this year and past years
- AI tips and tricks
- AI support tools
- Templates for your own work


## Our tracks
