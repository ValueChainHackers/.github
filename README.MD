# ValueChainHackers

ValueChainHackers is an open lab and a community for people who want to change supply chains.  

We take on real problems with companies, researchers, students, and communities. We look at what is broken, map it out, understand how the system works, and build ways to change it.  

When we say hacking, we mean getting past surface level. We get into the real system. We find the points where you can move things. We work through applied learning, with real people, building prototypes, testing, using modern tools like AI and network thinking.  

It is not theory. It is not endless talk. It is about giving people the skills, the urgency, and the community they need to drive real change.  

The platform is open source. The community is open. Anyone who wants to work is welcome.

## The Challenge: Wicked Problems in Supply Chains

Modern supply chains are full of wicked problems. These are complex, connected problems that cannot be solved with simple fixes. They come from a system that was built for cost and efficiency, not for sustainability or fairness.  

Climate change, human rights abuses, environmental damage. All of these are built into the way supply chains run today. They are locked into the relationships between suppliers, companies, governments, and communities.  

Most companies want to change this. Governments are asking them to. People want them to. But no one player can fix the system on their own. There is a gap between actors. There is no shared structure. No shared way to act.  

At the same time, the pressure is going up. In Europe, companies will soon need to prove that they meet strict standards on the environment and human rights. New laws will force them to understand and control risks across their entire chain. Most are not ready. Many do not even know where to start.  

That is the challenge we are working on.

## Our Approach: From Problem to Scalable Solution

ValueChainHackers is a lab where people work on these problems in the real world.  

We run an open 20-week program where mixed teams work on live challenges from companies, governments, or communities. These are not made-up cases. They are real problems that need solving.  

In this program, people do not sit in a room and talk about the problem. They work with stakeholders. They map out the system. They prototype fast. They test in the field. They use AI and automation to move faster.  

Each team works through eight steps:  
1. Problem identification  
2. Data collection  
3. Solution ideation  
4. Validation  
5. Proposal development  
6. Community engagement  
7. Testing  
8. Scaling  

At the end, they will have a prototype or a tested solution, and a path to scale it.  

They will also have built three key things:  
- Agency: I know what to do and how to do it  
- Urgency: I know why this matters and I care  
- Community: I know I am not alone  

## The Lab Platform

Everything we use in the lab is open source. All tools are modular and can be reused. Teams can take the stack back to their own company or community.  

We give people the tools to:
- Map supply chain networks  
- Analyze supplier relationships  
- Automate research and data collection with AI  
- Build dashboards and real-time monitoring  
- Prototype apps and tools  

The stack includes:
- AI chat and retrieval: Ollama, OpenWebUI  
- Automation: n8n  
- Dashboards and data: Grafana, Kotaemon, JupyterHub, RStudio  
- Graph database: Neo4j  
- Search and embeddings: Qdrant, SearXNG  
- Routing and orchestration: Traefik  

Teams do not need to be technical experts to use this. We help them learn fast. The point is not the tools, it is what you can do with them.

## Why Companies Work With Us

If you are a company, ValueChainHackers gives you a place to work on hard problems with a team. Problems like:
- How to trace raw materials
- How to lower scope 3 emissions
- How to reduce risks from suppliers
- How to comply with new regulations
- How to help small suppliers or improve livelihoods
- How to innovate around circularity  

We give you a team that will work full-time for 20 weeks on your challenge. These teams are mixed. They bring in students, researchers, community voices, and your own people. The team is guided and mentored the whole way.  

You do not get a report. You get a working prototype or tested solution. You get new ideas, new partners, and the chance to move faster.  

You also get to learn with other companies in the program. We do this openly. Everyone learns from everyone.  

This helps you stay ahead on sustainability and risk. It helps you meet ESG targets. It helps you comply with EU laws. It helps you avoid the costs of scandals or supply shocks.  

And it helps you tell a clear story to your customers and investors. That you are taking action.
