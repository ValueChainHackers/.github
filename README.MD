# ValueChainHackers

ValueChainHackers is an open lab and community for people who want to change supply chains.  

We take on real-world problems with companies, researchers, students, and communities. We map what is broken, surface power dynamics, and build and test new ways of working.  

Hacking means understanding how systems really work and finding leverage points for change. We do this through applied learning, stakeholder engagement, rapid prototyping, and AI-supported tools.  

It is not about theory. It is not about endless discussion. It is about building agency, urgency, and community, and delivering solutions that work in the real world.  

The stack is open source and modular. The work is shared. Anyone who wants to do the work is welcome.

## The problem

- Supply chains today are full of wicked problems  
- The world needs solutions that are:
  - socially and environmentally impactful
  - financially viable
  - scalable in practice  
- Many want to make a difference, but lack structure and support
  - Businesses
  - Institutions (government, certifiers)
  - Local communities

There is no coordination between these actors. That’s what we aim to change.


## How we work

We run a guided, applied program:

**20 weeks — from problem to scalable solution**  
- Real-world challenges  
- Business and community engagement  
- Mentorship, not theory  
- Applied learning with tools and tech  

We build:
- agency (I know what to do)
- urgency (I care why it matters)
- community (I’m not alone)


## What we build

We give you the tools to:

- Map supplier relationships
- Analyze supply chain networks
- Automate research tasks with AI
- Visualize complex chains
- Build open tools for real-world use


## What’s inside

- The full stack:  
  - AI chat + RAG: `Ollama`, `OpenWebUI`  
  - Automation: `n8n`  
  - Data & dashboards: `Grafana`, `Kotaemon`, `JupyterHub`, `RStudio`  
  - Graph database: `Neo4j`  
  - Search & embeddings: `Qdrant`, `SearXNG`  
  - Routing: `Traefik`

- Projects from this year and previous years
- AI tips & tricks for your work
- AI support tools
- Templates you can use in your own research or projects
